## Background

I am a freelance software engineer with a deep passion for large-scale open source projects. Over the years I have contributed to several open source CRM platforms — most notably VtigerCRM and SugarCRM — where my work touched historical codebases that many thousands of businesses rely on, even if much of that contribution went unacknowledged.

## Current Focus

My primary areas of expertise today are **CI/CD** (Continuous Integration and Continuous Deployment) and **Quality Assurance**. I have built extensive experience designing and maintaining automated pipelines that make software delivery faster, safer, and more reproducible. Alongside this specialisation I continue to write PHP and JavaScript, publishing my work openly so that other developers can learn from it and build upon it.

## Toolbox

The tools I reach for every day include **Docker**, **Bash**, and the major package managers. I use them to streamline development workflows and deployment processes, turning complex infrastructure concerns into repeatable, auditable steps.TestTest

## DevOps as a Culture

I think of myself as a *quasi-divulgatore* — a communicator and DevOps facilitator. Beyond writing code, I invest energy in bringing DevOps culture into organisations where its benefits are not yet obvious. My approach is deliberately simple and accessible: I focus on making abstract concepts concrete and on showing the tangible value that modern practices add to a team's day-to-day work.

## Mathematics

Mathematics is one of my deepest passions. I am fascinated by its rigour, its elegance, and the way a single well-chosen symbol can capture an infinite truth. This wiki is, in part, the expression of that fascination — a personal space where I study, collect results, and think carefully about the structures that underlie the world.

I carry with me a quiet but persistent dream: to contribute something of genuine value to mathematics. It need not be grand — a small, clean result, a useful observation, a proof that sheds new light on something familiar. That would be enough.

$\text{A small truth, clearly stated, is worth more than a large truth left unsaid.}$(5.1)
